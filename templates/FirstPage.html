Plan and log your meals

  1. Plan Your Meal
      Ensure a balance of macronutrients - carbohydrates, proteins, and fats. Include a variety of food groups such as fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, whole grains, and healthy fats.
  2. Learn Meal Nutrient
      Learn about portion sizes to avoid overeating. Balancing your intake of carbohydrates, proteins, and fats is crucial for a well-rounded diet.
  3. Log Your Meal
      Regularly review your food logs to monitor your progress towards your dietary goals. Adjust your meals as needed to stay on track.

Compare foods, ingredients, and recipes

  1. Nutritional Content
      Compare the nutritional profiles of different whole foods. Consider the levels of essential nutrients, including vitamins, minerals, protein, carbohydrates, and fats. Choose foods that align with your dietary goals.
  2. Find Your Recipes
      Evaluate the nutritional balance of recipes. A well-rounded recipe includes a mix of protein, carbohydrates, healthy fats, vitamins, and minerals.
  3. Health Benefits
      Assess the overall health benefits of different foods. For example, fatty fish like salmon provides omega-3 fatty acids, which are beneficial for heart health.
